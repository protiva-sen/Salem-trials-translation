SWP No. 13.6

(Indictment No. 5 of
Bridget Bishop
, for Afflicting
Ann Putnam Jr.
)
[+ June 2, 1692]
Anno Regni Regis et Reginae Willim et  Mariae nunc Angliae &'c Quarto:
Essex ss
The Juro'r for our Sovereigne Lord & Lady the King  & Queen p'rsent#[s] that
Bridgett Bishop
als Olliver the  wife of
Edward Bishop
of Salem in the County of Essex Sawyer the Nyneteenth  Day of April -- in the fourth Year of the Reigne of our Sovereigne Lord  & Lady William & Mary By the Grace of God of England Scottland  France & Ireland King & Queen Defend'r of the faith &'c and divers  other Dayes & times as well before as after. certaine Detestable Artes called Witchcrafts & Sorceries, Wickedly and felloniously hath used  Practised & Exercised at and within the Towneship of Salem, afores'd  in upon ag't one.
Ann puttnam
of Salem Village in the County afores'd singlewoman by which said wicked Arts the said
Ann puttnam
the  s'd Nyneteenth Day of April in the fourth Year aboves'd and  divers other Dayes & times as well before as after was & is hurt, tortured,  Afflicted Pined Consumed wasted & Tormented ag't the Peace of  our said Sovereigne Lord & Lady the King and Queen and against  the forme of the Statute in that Case made & Provided./.
Wittnesses
Ann puttnam Jun'r
M'r Sam'll paris
Nathan'll Ingersoll
Thomas puttnam
Mercy Lewis
Mary Walcott
Abigail Williams
Elizabeth Hubbard
(Reverse) (5) 5. Bills ag't
Brid't Bishop
alias Olliver
found by the Grand  Inquest

Billa vera
