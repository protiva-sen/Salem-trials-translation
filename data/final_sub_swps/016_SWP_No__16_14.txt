SWP No. 16.14

(Deposition of
Mary Walcott
v.
Mary Bradbury
)
[September 9, 1692 ]
[The depo]sistion of
Mary Walcott
who testifieth and saith that I being at Andevor on the later end of July 1692: and on the 26 day of the same [mo]nth I saw there
Mis mary Bradbery
the wife of
Capt Tho: Bradbery
of salisbury or hir Apperance most greviously affleting and tormenting of
Timothy Swan
of Andevor allmost Redy to kill him: also before and sence that time I have seen
mis Bradbery
or hir Apperance most greviously affleting and tormenting
Timothy Swan
and I doe beleve in my heart that
Mist. Bradbery
is a most  dreadffull wicth sence she has been in prison she or hir Apperance has come to me and most greviously tormented me
mary Walcot:
affirmed: the truth of the above written evidence before the Jury of Inquest upon oath #[Augus] Sept'r 9: 1692
(Reverse)
Mar[y Walcot]
( Essex County Court Archives, Salem - Witchcraft, vol. 2, no. 81. Massachusetts Supreme Judicial Court, Judicial Archives, Massachusetts State Archives, Boston, MA  )
