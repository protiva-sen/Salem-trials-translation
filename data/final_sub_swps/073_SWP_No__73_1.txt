SWP No. 73.1

( Deposition of
Clement Coldum
v.
Elizabeth Hubbard
)
[? June 3, 1692]
The deposition of
Clement Coldum
aged 60 years or ther about; saith that on the 29th: of May; 1692; being at Salem Village carrying home
Elizabeth Hubbard
from the Meeting behind me; she desired me to ride faster, I asked her why; she said the woods were full of Devils, & said ther & there they be, but I could se none; then I put on my horse, & after I had rid a while, she told me I might ride softer, for we had out rid them. I asked her if she was not afraid of the Devil, she answered me no, she could discourse with the Devil as well as with me, & further saith not; this I am ready to testifie on Oath if called thereto, as witness my hand;
#[Clement Coldum].
*Clement Colddom
Against
Eliz: Hubbard
( Essex County Court Archives, Salem -- Witchcraft Vol. 2, no. 122, Massachusetts Supreme Judicial Court, Judicial Archives, Massachusetts State Archives, Boston, MA. )
