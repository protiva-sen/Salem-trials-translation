SWP No. 24.2

(Warrant for apprehension of
Martha Carrier
, and Officer's Return )
[May 28, 1692 ]
To the Marshall of Essex or his dept or to the Constables of Andover
You are in theire Majests names hereby required [to] apprehend  and forthwith secure, and bring before [us]
martha Carrier
the wife  of Thomas Currier of An[dover] on Tuesday next being the 31't  day of this Instant mo[nth] of May about ten of the clock in the forenoon or as soon as may be afterwards at [the] house of L't
Nathaniell Ingersalls
in Salem Village who stands charged with have-  ing Committed Sundry [acts] of Witchcraft on the Bodys of
Mary Walcot
and
abi[gail] williams
of Salem Village to theire great hurt  & [injury] in order to her Examination Relateing to the prem[ises]  abovesaid faile not
Dated Salem  May 28'th 1692.
per us
*John Hathorne
*Jonathan.Corwin
Assis'ts
(Reverse) I have apprehend the w'tin named parson
and brought her  to the place appinted --
by me
*John Ballard.
const andover
( Essex County Court Archives, Salem - Witchcraft. Vol. 1, no. 310. Massachusetts Supreme Judicial Court, Judicial Archives, Massachusetts State Archives, MA. )
