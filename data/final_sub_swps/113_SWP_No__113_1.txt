SWP No. 113.1

(Warrant for the apprehension of
Ann Pudeator
and
Alice Parker
, & Officer's Return )
[May 12, 1692]
To the Marshall of Essex or Constable  in Salem
You are in theire Majes'ts names hereby required forthwith to apprehend and bring before us
Allce. Parker
the wife of
John Parker
of Salem and
Ann Pudeater
of Salem Widdow who stand Charged with Sundry acts of Witchcraft by them Committed this day Contrary to the Laws of our. Sov'r Lord & Lady faile not Dated Salem May the .12'th 1692
per us
*John: Hathorne
] Assis'ts
*Jonathan. Corwin
] Assis'ts
May 12'th -- 1692 I have apprehended the above named persons and Brought them att the place apointed by your honers
Per mee
*George Herrick
Marshall of Essex
(Reverse) Warrant
( Essex County Court Archives, Salem -- Witchcraft Vol. 1, no. 261, Massachusetts Supreme Judicial Court, Judicial Archives, Massachusetts State Archives, Boston, MA. )
